  • The term "atomic bomb" first appeared in the 1940s, when the United States was developing the first nuclear weapons for use in World War II.
  • The term "atomic" at the time referred to the fact that the atom, which people today understand significantly more than they did at the time, was the source of the bomb's destructive force.
  • The phrase "nuclear bomb" gained popularity after the war because it more precisely defined the technology underlying the lethal weapon.
  • Although both phrases are still in use today, "nuclear bomb" is frequently regarded as the more precise and scientific term.
